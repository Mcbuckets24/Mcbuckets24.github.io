What makes the manga medium special? Honestly I don't know. There's something that it's able to capture that I feel others cannot. The diversity from the longevity of something like One Piece, the beauty of a Vagabond or Berserk, all the way to the slop of shitty isekais. Manga is just all over the place, but somewhere in that mess is something profound. Something that, for me, speaks to th human experience, the desire to create art, and the ability to leave lasting impacts through stories that mean more than words and pictures could express alone. As for me, I've become a different reader over the past few years. I've sunk into the seinen hole, where I've found it to be most comfortable. Some of my favorites are: A Silent Voice, 20th Century Boys, Vagabond, and One Piece. Similarly, my favorite mangaka are: Noaki Urasawa, Takehiko Inoue, and Yoshitoki Oima. I try my best to cover most of what I find interesting, but there's definitely some gaps in my knowledge here and there. Hit me up with any recommendations, the backlog can always get longer...
